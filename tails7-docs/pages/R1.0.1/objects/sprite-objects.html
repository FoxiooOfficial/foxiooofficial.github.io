Sprite Objects

29 November 2025

Sprites are objects that are located in the 3D.Object Group and those that are mapped on the screen. They can serve as points for Polygon Objects as well as Line Objects.

These objects form the foundation of Mode 7 genesis; the background itself, which is the Tails7 Ground Render, is not enough - we need some objects or points to create the illusion of three-dimensionality. Therefore, there are sprites that can obtain any position in space.

Tails7 is a modular engine, meaning that additional modes can be easily added for how each sprite should behave individually - here for screen display, here for scale, here for layering, or even rotation. All modes for sprites are specified in the prepared Alterable String, namely: _MappingScreenMode, _MappingMode, _DistanceMode, _ScaleMode, _LayerMode, _VisibleMode and _RotationMode (Descriptions of these variables are at the bottom of the page).

Sprites have pseudo *4 position vectors: _PositionX and _PositionY are the actual positions, while _OffsetX and _OffsetY are the object's offsets on the screen. (_OffsetY can also be referred to as the Z position)



These objects can also be scaled separately for the X and Y axes using the variables _ScaleX, _ScaleY, and _Scale, where the latter multiplies X and Y.
(For example: _ScaleX is 5, _ScaleY is 10, and _Scale is 2; the final scale result is 5 * 2 = 10 for the X axis, 10 * 2 = 20 for the Y axis.)



Sprites can have individual layering, meaning when a given object should be displayed under or above another object. The easiest way to set this is with the _LayerOffset variable.



And changing the object's angle is declared in the _RotationMod variable.
